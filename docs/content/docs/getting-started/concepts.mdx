## 3D Chromatin Conformation Capture

Chromatin conformation capture technologies reveal how DNA is organized in three-dimensional space within the nucleus.

## Hi-C

**Hi-C** captures genome-wide chromatin interactions without protein specificity.

- Unbiased, genome-wide contact map
- Reveals TADs, compartments, and loops
- Requires 1-10M cells

## ChIA-PET

**ChIA-PET** combines ChIP with proximity ligation for protein-specific interactions.

- Protein-specific interactions (CTCF, RNAPII, cohesin)
- High signal-to-noise ratio
- Requires 10-50M cells

## HiChIP

**HiChIP** combines Hi-C with ChIP for low-input protein-specific mapping.

- Protein-specific interactions
- Low input (100K-1M cells)
- Cost-effective alternative to ChIA-PET

## Key Differences

| Feature | Hi-C | ChIA-PET | HiChIP |
|---------|------|----------|---------|
| Input cells | 1-10M | 10-50M | 100K-1M |
| Protein specificity | No | Yes | Yes |
| Best for | Genome architecture | Specific proteins | Low-input loops |
