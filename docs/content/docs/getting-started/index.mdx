## 🏗️ The Structures You'll Find

Hi-C reveals that chromosomes aren't random tangles—they have organized structures at multiple scales.

### A/B Compartments



The genome separates into "A" compartments (open, active genes) and "B" compartments (closed, silent genes). Think of it like oil and water separating in the nucleus.

### TADs (Topologically Associating Domains)



These are neighborhoods where DNA interacts mostly with itself, typically 200kb-1Mb in size. Enhancers usually only talk to promoters in the same TAD.

### Chromatin Loops



Direct contacts between specific genomic sites, often bringing enhancers and promoters together. These are the details that matter for gene regulation.

### Chromosome Territories



Each chromosome lives in its own region of the nucleus. Interactions between different chromosomes are rare—which is why you see a strong diagonal in Hi-C contact maps.

## The Molecular Players

Structures don't just appear magically. Here are the proteins doing the heavy lifting:

### CTCF - The Boundary Guard


This protein sits at TAD boundaries like a stop sign, blocking loop extrusion. About 33,000 binding sites across the human genome. When CTCF is gone, boundaries dissolve.

### Cohesin - The Loop Builder

    

A ring-shaped complex that literally extrudes chromatin loops. It loads onto DNA, starts pushing out a loop, and keeps going until it hits CTCF or falls off. The whole process takes just a few minutes.

### The Loop Extrusion Model



This is the current best explanation for how TADs and loops form. It's elegant, it's dynamic, and you can actually see it happening in live cells now.

### Other Key Regulators

YY1, Mediator, LDB1—there's a whole cast of characters that organize chromatin in cohesin-independent ways.

## 🎯 Why This Matters: Gene Regulation in 3D

The 3D organization of chromosomes isn't just structurally interesting—it controls which genes turn on or off.

### Enhancer-Promoter Communication



Enhancers can be hundreds of kilobases away from the genes they regulate. Loops bring them together in 3D space.

### Boundaries Keep Things Organized



TAD boundaries prevent enhancers from accidentally activating the wrong gene. When boundaries break (like in cancer), chaos ensues.

### Structure Follows Function



Active genes cluster together in active compartments. Silent genes do the same. The 3D architecture reflects and reinforces transcriptional states.

### Everything Changes



During mitosis, all loops dissolve. After cell division, they reform in about 20-60 minutes. During differentiation, the whole 3D landscape reorganizes to support new gene expression programs.

## 📊 Reading Your Data

Now for the practical part—understanding what you're looking at.

### Contact Maps 101



Learn to read Hi-C heatmaps. Diagonal = nearby stuff touching (expected). Off-diagonal dots = loops (interesting). Triangles = TADs. Checkerboard = compartments.

### Resolution vs Sequencing Depth



Higher resolution is great, but it costs you. For human genomes: ~100M reads gets you TADs, ~6B reads gets you fine loops. Choose wisely based on your question and budget.

### Pattern Recognition



Dots, stripes, triangles, plaid—each pattern tells a biological story. Learn what they mean and what they reveal about chromatin organization.
